When and where was Valerie Harper born?
Valerie Harper was born on August 22, 1939, in Suffern, New York, USA.
How many children did Valerie Harper have?
She had one child, a daughter named Cristina Cacciotti, born in 1983. Cristina's father is Valerie Harper's second husband, Tony Cacciotti.
How many marriages did Valerie Harper have?
Valerie Harper was married twice. Her first marriage was to Richard Schaal, an actor and comedian, in 1964. They divorced in 1978. Her second marriage was to Tony Cacciotti, whom she married in 1987 and remained married to until her passing in 2019. Tony Cacciotti was also known for being her dedicated caregiver during her battle with cancer.
What is Valerie Harper best known for in the world of television?
Valerie Harper is best known for her portrayal of Rhoda Morgenstern on "The Mary Tyler Moore Show" and its spin-off series, "Rhoda."
How many Emmy Awards did Valerie Harper win for her role as Rhoda Morgenstern?
Valerie Harper won four Emmy Awards for her role as Rhoda Morgenstern on "The Mary Tyler Moore Show" and "Rhoda."
What other notable TV series did Valerie Harper appear in besides "The Mary Tyler Moore Show" and "Rhoda"?
Valerie Harper also appeared in TV series such as "Valerie," "The Hogan Family," and "Dancing with the Stars."
Did Valerie Harper have a successful career in film as well?
While Valerie Harper was primarily known for her television work, she also appeared in several films, including "Freebie and the Bean" and "Blame It on Rio."
What health challenge did Valerie Harper face in her later years?
Valerie Harper was diagnosed with terminal brain cancer, specifically leptomeningeal carcinomatosis, in 2013. She defied the odds and continued to live and work for several years after the diagnosis.
Did Valerie Harper write a memoir about her life and career?
Yes, Valerie Harper wrote a memoir titled "I, Rhoda," which was published in 2013. The book reflects on her life, career, and her battle with cancer.
When did Valerie Harper pass away?
Valerie Harper passed away on August 30, 2019, at the age of 80. She left behind a legacy of beloved television characters and a career that spanned several decades.
